    1.

    Four students are running for president of their residence hall: Debra (D), Farah (F), Jorge (J), and Hillary (H). The votes of their fellow students are summarized in the following preferences table. Who is declared the president using the plurality method?
    🔗
    Number of Votes 52 45 13 9 4
    First choice D F J F H
    Second choice F J F J J
    Third choice H H H D D
    Fourth choice J D D H F
    Solution.
    Under the plurality method, we only consider first-choice votes:
    • Debra (D): 52 votes
      🔗
      🔗
    • Farah (F): 45 + 9 = 54 votes
      🔗
      🔗
    • Jorge (J): 13 votes
      🔗
      🔗
    • Hillary (H): 4 votes
      🔗
      🔗
    Farah has the highest number of first-choice votes (54). Farah is declared the president.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    2.

    Four students are running for president of their residence hall: Debra (D), Farah (F), Jorge (J), and Hillary (H). The votes of their fellow students are summarized in the following preferences table. Who is declared the president using the plurality-with-elimination method?
    🔗
    Number of Votes 52 45 13 9 4
    First choice D F J F H
    Second choice F J F J J
    Third choice H H H D D
    Fourth choice J D D H F
    Solution.
    Total votes cast = 52 + 45 + 13 + 9 + 4 = 123 votes. A majority requires 62 votes to win outright.
    🔗
    Round 1: D has 52, F has 54, J has 13, H has 4. No candidate holds a majority. Hillary (H) is eliminated. Her 4 votes transfer to Jorge (J).
    🔗
    Round 2: D has 52, F has 54, J has 13 + 4 = 17. Still no majority. Jorge (J) is eliminated. His 13 primary ballots move to Farah. The 4 ballots from H move to Debra.
    🔗
    Round 3: D has 56 votes. F has 67 votes. Farah is declared the president.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    3.

    Four students are running for president of their residence hall: Debra (D), Farah (F), Jorge (J), and Hillary (H). The votes of their fellow students are summarized in the following preferences table. Who is declared the president using the Borda count method?
    🔗
    Number of Votes 52 45 13 9 4
    First choice D F J F H
    Second choice F J F J J
    Third choice H H H D D
    Fourth choice J D D H F
    Solution.
    Points assignment: 1st choice = 4 pts, 2nd choice = 3 pts, 3rd choice = 2 pts, 4th choice = 1 pt.
    • Debra (D): \(208 + 45 + 13 + 18 + 8 = 292\) points.
      🔗
      🔗
    • Farah (F): \(156 + 180 + 39 + 36 + 4 = 415\) points.
      🔗
      🔗
    • Jorge (J): \(52 + 135 + 52 + 27 + 12 = 278\) points.
      🔗
      🔗
    • Hillary (H): \(104 + 90 + 26 + 9 + 16 = 245\) points.
      🔗
      🔗
    Farah has the highest point total. Farah is declared the president.
    🔗
    🔗

    4.

    Four students are running for president of their residence hall: Debra (D), Farah (F), Jorge (J), and Hillary (H). The votes of their fellow students are summarized in the following preferences table. Who is declared the president using the pairwise comparison method? Is there a Condorcet winner?
    🔗
    Number of Votes 52 45 13 9 4
    First choice D F J F H
    Second choice F J F J J
    Third choice H H H D D
    Fourth choice J D D H F
    Solution.
    We analyze head-to-head match-ups:
    • D vs F: D gets 52, F gets 71. (F gets 1 pt)
      🔗
      🔗
    • D vs J: D gets 52, J gets 71. (J gets 1 pt)
      🔗
      🔗
    • D vs H: D gets 65, H gets 58. (D gets 1 pt)
      🔗
      🔗
    • F vs J: F gets 106, J gets 17. (F gets 1 pt)
      🔗
      🔗
    • F vs H: F gets 106, H gets 17. (F gets 1 pt)
      🔗
      🔗
    • J vs H: J gets 71, H gets 52. (J gets 1 pt)
      🔗
      🔗
    Final Match Points: Farah (F) = 3, Jorge (J) = 2, Debra (D) = 1, Hillary (H) = 0. Farah wins the pairwise matchup. Because Farah defeats all other candidates head-to-head, Farah is the Condorcet winner.
    🔗
