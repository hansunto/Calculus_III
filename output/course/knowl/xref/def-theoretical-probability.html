Definition 3.1. Probability Formula.

The theoretical probability of an event is a measure of how likely that event is to occur based on the assumption that all outcomes in the sample space are equally likely.
🔗
If an event E has \(n(E)\) equally likely outcomes and its sample space S has \(n(S)\) equally likely outcomes, the probability of event, \(P(E)\text{,}\) is
\begin{gather*} P(E) = \frac{\text{number of outcomes in event } E}{\text{total number of outcomes in sample space}} = \frac{n(E)}{n(S)} \end{gather*}
