Item 4.
Suppose a 2D curve is given parametrically by \(x=f(t)\) and \(y=g(t)\text{,}\) where \(f''\) and \(g''\) exist on an interval \(I\text{.}\) Since the slope of the tangent line is \(\tan \theta = \frac{g'(t)}{f'(t)}\) for \(f'(t) \neq 0\text{,}\) differentiating \(\theta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{g'(t)}{f'(t)}\right)\) with respect to \(t\) and dividing by \(\frac{ds}{dt}\) yields:
\begin{equation*} \kappa =\frac{|f'(t)g''(t)-g'(t)f''(t)|}{\left[(f'(t))^2+(g'(t))^2\right]^{3/2}} \end{equation*}
